Your AI Demo Is Not a Business Case

Capability, behavior, operations, and money are four different currencies. Value only counts at the rung where your CFO lives, and accuracy doesn't convert at par.

The scene repeats in every company. The demo dazzles. The deck says 96% accuracy. Someone multiplies hours saved by average salary and announces two million dollars of value. The CFO asks which budget line will shrink, and the room goes quiet. Nothing in that deck was false. It was just denominated in the wrong currency.

The Value Ladder

Four rungs. Each one is necessary for the rung above it, and none of them guarantees it.

RUNG 1CapabilityCurrency: demo metrics

Measures: accuracy, quality scores, eval pass rates, "it got the right answer."

Proves: the model can do the work. Cannot prove: anyone will let it.

↑

Converts only if people change how they work. Most accuracy never does.

RUNG 2BehaviorCurrency: adoption metrics

Measures: weekly active users, share of cases routed through the AI, retention after week six, who quietly went back to the old way.

Proves: people changed how they work. Cannot prove: the work got better.

↑

Converts only if usage moves a workflow number. Busy tools can move nothing.

RUNG 3OperationsCurrency: workflow metrics

Measures: cycle time, throughput, error rate, rework, SLA performance, against the baseline written before the build.

Proves: the workflow improved. Cannot prove: the business captured it.

↑

Converts only if someone redeploys the gain. Unclaimed capacity evaporates.

RUNG 4MoneyCurrency: P&L metrics

Measures: cost per transaction, revenue per head, volume absorbed without hiring, a budget line that actually changed.

Proves: the business changed. This is the only rung a CFO can spend.

The hours-saved trap

The most common business case in AI is also the most fictional:

2,000 users × 3 hrs/week × $60/hr = $18.7M of "value"

Nobody's cost went down. Nobody's output went up. The three hours dissolved into the workday: a longer lunch, a cleaner inbox, an earlier Friday. Hours saved is not money saved. It is capacity created, and capacity has no value until somebody reassigns it to revenue work, absorbed volume, or a smaller hiring plan.

The honest version of that business case names the reassignment: "support absorbed 30% more tickets with the same team." Same AI. Different rung. Only one of them survives a CFO.

Measure all four rungs, but report each in its own currency. The fatal move is presenting rung 1 numbers as if they were rung 4 money. That's how AI programs lose the CFO — not by failing, but by claiming value in a currency the P&L doesn't accept.

How to Use This

STEP 1

Place your metrics

Sort every number you report onto its rung. Most decks live entirely on rung 1.

STEP 2

Instrument 2 and 3

Adoption and workflow metrics need plumbing. Build it before launch, not after.

STEP 3

Name the conversion

For every rung-3 gain, write down who redeploys it and into what.

STEP 4

Report honestly

Four currencies, four lines in the readout. Never exchange them silently.
