AI Value = Technology × Process × Adoption

It's a product, not a sum — miss any factor and the value goes to zero. Most AI stalls because it ships technology with no process or adoption. I deliver all three. Below: two production AI use cases at Intuit — built, governed, and measured — plus the framework they generalize into.

Transformation starts with disciplined selection: pick the workflow where friction is highest, frequency is constant, and slow coordination is most visible — then turn it into a governed operating system. Both use cases below were chosen on exactly these criteria.

Agentic AI · 8-Agent System · Program Operations

Multi-Agent Program Operations System

DEPLOYED · MEASURED · INTUIT
What the PMO got: leadership-update readiness dropped from ~10 hrs/week to under 30 minutes — freeing the team for decisions, with always-current, risk-flagged status.

The high-friction problem

A 12-person GTM PMO across two functional groups spent ~10 hrs/week manually assembling leadership updates from scattered sources — Drive, Slack, email, Zoom — and rebuilding the same reports by hand. Status was stale by the time leadership reviewed it. High friction, every single week.

Technology × Process × Adoption

Technology

A multi-agent system — 8 specialized AI agents integrated across Drive, Slack, Zoom, Outlook & GitHub — scans, drafts, and maintains a live program binder on schedule.

Process

Governance I designed: human-in-the-loop approvals, commit-gated changes, audited memory — 6 leadership cadences run reliably.

Adoption

Rolled out to all 12 users via training, working sessions, and iterative fixes from real-run feedback.

AI Governance · Quality at Scale

PMM Reporting Intelligence & Governance

DEPLOYED · MEASURED · INTUIT
What the PMMs got: their time back for actual GTM work — ~2–3 hrs/week each (~16–24 hrs across the team) — instead of hand-producing progress updates for 6–8 operating mechanisms, with every update auto-held to a 9-point leadership quality bar.

The high-friction problem

Across 8 PMMs and 11 GTM initiatives, the team hand-produced progress updates for 6–8 recurring operating mechanisms every cycle — in inconsistent formats and quality. Leadership sent updates back for the "so what," missing numbers, or unclear risk plans, and PMMs spent hours on reporting instead of actual marketing work.

Technology × Process × Adoption

Technology

An AI engine that ingests progress in any format and auto-standardizes it into a leadership-ready update.

Process

A 9-point leadership quality rubric, enforced on every update — governance is the product, not an afterthought.

Adoption

In live use by 8 PMMs across 11 initiatives — the standard the whole team writes to.

The 9-point quality bar it enforces

✓"So what" stated first
✓Acronyms expanded for non-Payments readers
✓Actionable, results-oriented detail
✓Wins & results with numbers + dates
✓Risks & issues with impact + plan
✓Next steps with clear ETA
✓Blockers called out
✓Path to Green for any Red/Yellow
✓Decisions documented
